 Assignment Questions (One per Concept)



1. INNER JOIN

 Question:

# Display employee name, department name, and location for employees who are assigned to a department.

 Purpose: Shows matching records in both tables.



2. LEFT JOIN

 Question:

# Display all employees along with their department name.  
# Include employees who are not assigned to any department.

 Purpose: Understand NULL handling on the right table.



 3. RIGHT JOIN

 Question:

# Display all departments along with employee names working in them.  
# Include departments even if no employees are assigned.

 Purpose: Opposite of LEFT JOIN.



 4. FULL OUTER JOIN

 Question:

# Display all employees and all departments, showing matching records where available and NULL otherwise.

 Purpose: Understand complete data coverage.


If FULL OUTER JOIN is not supported, use:

   #LEFT JOIN#
   #RIGHT JOIN#
   #UNION#



5. SELF JOIN

 Question:

# Display employee name and their manager name.

 Purpose: Joining a table with itself using aliases.



 6. SUBQUERY (Single‑Row Subquery)

 Question:

# Find employees who earn more than the average salary of all employees.

 Purpose: Using a subquery inside #WHERE#.



 7. CORRELATED SUBQUERY

 Question:

# Find employees who earn more than the average salary of their own department.

 Purpose: Subquery depends on outer query row.



 8. SUBQUERY LIST (IN / NOT IN)

 Question:

# Display employees who work in departments that have projects assigned.

 Purpose: Subquery returns multiple rows.



9.Query:

   Find departments with no employees
   Find managers who manage more than 2 employees
   Find the highest paid employee per department
